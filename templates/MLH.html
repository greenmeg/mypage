Michigan Legal Help

A legal self-help website.

>

I worked with a team of 4 as a UX researcher for Michigan Legal Help for SI 622: Needs ASsessment & Usability Evaluation. Over the course of the Winter 2016 semester we conducted a usability analysis of their website. Michigan Legal Help is currently using our findings in their website redesign.


Client: Michigan Legal Help

Date: Jan 2016 - April 2016

Team: Erin Lilly, Vishnu Kumaar Mahendran, & Jing Wang

Roles: Project Manager, User Experience Researcher

Tools: Interaction map, interviews, personas & scenarios, comparative analysis, surveys, heuristic evauation, usability testing & user interviews

OUR CLIENT

Michigan Legal Help is an online legal self‐help website, created to help people manage simple civil legal problems without the assistance of a lawyer. The website is most commonly used by those having legal issues concerning family, abuse, or housing. One challenge our client seeks to address is the accessibility of legal resources for low-income users who lack a college education. Michigan Legal Help provides toolkits, forms, and information about community resources.

INTERVIEWS

We began with meeting our client in person to get an idea of their needs, goals, and expectations.
Afterwards, we interviewed seven possible users, three of which were held at the 15th District Court. This interviews gave us an understanding of what users expect in a legal self-help resource, as well as how they currently find legal information. We used these findings to create three personas and scenarios, which helped us empathize with user needs.

COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

MLH provides an impressive array of resources, but through comparative analysis of similar resources such as LegalZoom and even public law libraries, we discovered new ways in which MLH could make their experience better.

SURVEYS

We created a survey which was distributed on the MLH’s webpage. We garnered 86 responses, which helped us understand how real users interacted with the site.

HEURISTICS

Each member conducted a heuristic evaluation based on Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics. Afterwards, we consolidated our findings and prioritized them based on severity on a scale from 0-4.

USER TESTING

Finally, we conducted seven usability tests with users matching our target demographic between the age of 30-50 years old, with a slight majority of women. Each test lasted approximately one hour, and included two scenarios and nine tasks. These findings clarified the navigation difficulties faced by MLH’s average user.
