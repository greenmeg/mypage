I signed an NDA!

During the Winter semester of 2016 I worked with a team of 3 on an EA title with EA's Redwood campus under the guidance of Apoorva Bansal. While we analyzed their game we focused on delightful factors in mobile games for children, as well as purchase intent from parents.


Client: Electronic Arts

Date: Jan 2016 - April 2016

Team: Jim Stanhope, Jeff Zhang, & Vispan Boocha

Roles: User Experience Researcher

Tools: User interviewing & testing, heuristic evaluation, personas, research, brainstorming

RESEARCH

Before diving into the game, I took it upon myself to research child psychology in relation to gaming, playtesting, and learning. My background as an educator jumpstarted my research. I then reported my findings to my team, educating them on how child psychology would affect our analysis of the game.

PLAYTESTING

We held multiple seven playtesting sessions with our market audience of parents of young children. We recorded each session and payed close attention to the various pain points. After each session we interviewed the user to gain a better understanding of their experience with the game.

ANALYSIS

After 3 weeks of user testing, we held a brainstorming session to analyze our findings. Finally, we agreed upon necessary changes to the game to allow for a better gaming experience.

DELIVERABLES

Our team drafted a report of our official findings. We then presented these findings to our team at Electronic Arts. These findings will be used to guide future research with new builds.
